Project Description

Okir to Glyph is a poster designed during a contemporary graphic design course at Aalto University. The course assignment was to combine something new with something old, in addition to utilizing methods learned during the course. For this project I went out of my comfort zone and designed in a way that is not typical for me: using bold colors and taking inspiration from my Filipino side. The inspiration for this poster was taken from Okir, which is a term for curvy plant-based designs and folk motifs among the Moro and Lumad peoples of the Philippines.

I wanted to recreate the shapes of Okir with something new, so I drew the shapes and the letters using Glyphdrawing.club. I combined historical filipino art with a relatively new digital tool to create a contemporary art poster. During the process, I experimented with different colors and compositions in order to achieve a contemporary design aesthetic while paying homage to filipino culture.

Experimenting with different color combinations. The goal was to avoid my typical color palette of black and white completely. This poster works with multiple color combinations and gradients even though I ended up with pink and orange.
