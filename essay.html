Project Description

The issue with contemporary digital portfolio platforms is how it makes the design process invisible and reduces visible design work into easily digestible bites. Style becomes more important than knowledge, context, or collaboration, which creates an issue for many designers including myself. Through this essay, I rethink and redesign a new concept for a portfolio, which fulfills both my needs and the needs of observers, followers and possible employers. Prisoner of My Own Portfolio is a personal essay on porfolio anxiety.

In addition to writing this essay, I designed the layout and typography. I was inspired by typography in the source material, and wanted to continue the same style in my own essay. As a designer, it is important to design the layout as these are my words, my thoughts and my story. I usually want to control the story.
